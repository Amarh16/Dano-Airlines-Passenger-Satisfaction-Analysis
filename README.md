# Dano-Airlines-Passenger-Satisfaction-Analysis

Analysed 129K airline passenger records to uncover key drivers of customer satisfaction. Evaluated travel class, trip purpose, and flight delays to provide recommendations for improving service quality, customer retention, and operational performance.


![](https://github.com/Amarh16/Dano-Airlines-Passenger-Satisfaction-Analysis/blob/main/IMG_1665.jpg)


## Executive Summary

This analysis was conducted to investigate the decline in passenger satisfaction at Dano Airlines. The objective was to identify the key factors influencing customer satisfaction and provide recommendations that management can implement to improve the passenger experience.

The dashboard analysed passenger satisfaction, travel behaviour, class preferences, and the impact of flight delays on customer experience.

The results reveal that overall passenger satisfaction is low, with less than half of customers reporting a satisfactory experience. The analysis suggests that travel purpose, cabin class, and delays significantly influence passenger satisfaction levels.

### Interpretation

The dashboard shows that Dano Airlines served approximately 129,000 passengers. Only 43.4% of passengers reported being satisfied with their experience. This indicates that more than half of customers were either dissatisfied or neutral, which confirms management’s concern regarding declining customer satisfaction.

Passengers experienced an average delay of approximately 15 minutes, which may seem small but can significantly impact customer perception, particularly for business travellers. The average flight distance of 1,190 miles suggests a mixture of short-haul and medium-haul routes.

## 2. Satisfaction Overview

### Findings

- Satisfied passengers: 56.26K (43.45%)
- Neutral or Dissatisfied passengers: 73.23K (56.55%)

### Insight

- The majority of passengers are not fully satisfied with their airline experience
- The fact that dissatisfied or neutral passengers exceed satisfied passengers by approximately 17,000 customers is a major concern
- This indicates that service delivery is not consistently meeting passenger expectations

### Business Impact

Low satisfaction levels can lead to:

- Reduced customer loyalty
- Negative online reviews
- Lower repeat bookings
- Increased customer acquisition costs

## 3. Satisfaction by Travel Type

### Findings

- Business Travel Satisfaction Rate: Approximately 41.63% satisfied
- Personal Travel Satisfaction Rate: Approximately 89.87% satisfied

### Insight

Passengers travelling for personal reasons appear significantly more satisfied than those travelling for business purposes.

Business travellers typically have higher expectations regarding:

- Punctuality
- Convenience
- Boarding efficiency
- WiFi availability
- Airport services

Any disruption in these areas tends to have a stronger impact on business travellers.

### Business Impact

- Business travellers are often higher-value customers who travel more frequently
- Poor satisfaction among this group may result in lost corporate contracts and reduced long-term revenue

## 4. Satisfaction by Travel Class

### Findings

Business Class:

- Highest number of satisfied passengers

Economy Class:

- Largest passenger segment
- Significant number of dissatisfied passengers

Economy Plus:

- Lowest passenger volume

### Insight

Passengers travelling in premium cabins generally report better experiences.

Business Class customers benefit from:

- More comfortable seating
- Better service quality
- Priority boarding
- Improved in-flight experience

Economy passengers experience lower satisfaction levels, suggesting that service quality gaps may exist in the standard cabin experience.

### Business Impact

Improving the Economy Class experience could have the greatest impact on overall satisfaction because this cabin represents the largest passenger group.

## 5. Impact of Flight Delays on Satisfaction

### Findings

The scatter plot demonstrates a clear positive relationship between:

- Departure Delay
- Arrival Delay
- Flights that depart late generally arrive late.

A noticeable concentration of dissatisfied passengers appears among flights with longer delays.

### Insight

Flight delays are one of the strongest drivers of passenger dissatisfaction.

Passengers often associate delays with:

- Poor operational efficiency
- Missed connections
- Increased travel stress
- Reduced trust in the airline

### Business Impact

- Repeated delays can damage the airline’s reputation and influence future booking decisions
- Improving operational punctuality would likely increase overall customer satisfaction

## Overall Conclusions

The analysis confirms that Dano Airlines is currently experiencing a customer satisfaction challenge.

Three major drivers of dissatisfaction were identified:

1. Low Overall Satisfaction: 
Only 43.4% of passengers report satisfaction. This means the majority of customers leave with a neutral or negative perception of the airline

2. Travel Segment Differences: 
Business travellers appear less satisfied than leisure travellers. This indicates that Dano Airlines may not be fully meeting the expectations of its most valuable customer segment.

3. Operational Delays: 
Flight delays have a direct impact on customer satisfaction. Passengers experiencing delays are more likely to report dissatisfaction.

## Recommendations

### Recommendation 1: Improve Flight Punctuality

Priority Level: High

Actions:

- Improve scheduling efficiency
- Reduce turnaround times
- Enhance aircraft maintenance planning
- Monitor delay root causes

Expected Outcome:

- Reduced passenger frustration
- Improved satisfaction scores
- Better customer retention

### Recommendation 2: Focus on Business Travellers

Priority Level: High

Actions:

- Improve airport lounge access
- Enhance WiFi quality
- Streamline boarding processes
- Introduce business traveller loyalty incentives

Expected Outcome:

- Increased corporate customer retention
- Improved premium revenue streams

### Recommendation 3: Enhance Economy Class Experience

Priority Level: Medium

Actions:

- Improve seat comfort
- Enhance food and beverage offerings
- Improve cabin cleanliness consistency
- Provide better onboard entertainment

Expected Outcome:

- Increased satisfaction across the largest customer segment

### Recommendation 4: Establish Customer Feedback Monitoring

Priority Level: Medium

Actions:

- Conduct post-flight surveys
- Monitor satisfaction trends monthly
- Track recurring complaints
- Create service improvement scorecards

Expected Outcome:

- Faster identification of service issues
- Continuous improvement culture

## Final Business Recommendation
Dano Airlines should prioritise operational reliability and customer experience improvements, particularly for business travellers and Economy Class passengers. By reducing delays and enhancing service quality, the airline can significantly improve passenger satisfaction, strengthen customer loyalty, and increase long-term profitability.
